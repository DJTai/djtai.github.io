Åpmam na Manugi' Yu' Guini

Been Awhile Since I've Wrote Here

By David Taitingfong | January 14, 2023 | 2 minutes
lina'la' todu chamoru

Hu prumeti hamyu na ti maleffa yu' nu ini na “blog” 😁. Ginin i ma’pus na Huliu, hu gof na’siguru na siña yu' manugi' guini sa' masangåni yu' na ti siña pumo’lu iyo-ku na kod gi publikun sagan kod1, yan kåo un tungu' månu nai hu po’lu ini na “blog”?…

Ha’a, dinanchi!…gi publikun sagan kod! 🙃 Ya pues ti manugi' “blog” asta ki sen siguru yu' na siña manugi' yu' guini!

Låo anyway - håfa manatatmanu hamyu?! Måolek yu' yan magof lokkui' sa' gi halacha mama’tinas yu' ottru na “account” gi Instagram yan manugi' ha' yan fuminu' ha' Chamoru guihi - siña un li’i' (yan tattiyi lokkui' an malago'-mu) gi i.dihan.dabit. Ti gef meggai na “posts” trabiha, låo siempri bei po’lu mås! 🫡

Guaha ottru lokkui': bei tutuhun iyo-ku prugraman “Masters in Computer Science” gi tinituhon-ña Måtsu 🤓. Gof apurao yu' låo dididi' na chathinassu lokkui' sa' siempri bei tinani'!

Ai lokkui'…måolekña i baban guaha ki nu i maolek tåya' 😅.

Ennåo ha' para på’gu. Bula guinaiya! — DJT.

Pinila' / English Translation

I promise y’all that I haven’t forgot about this blog 😁. Since last July, I’ve been making sure that I could write here because I was told that I couldn’t host my code in public repos, and do you know where I put this blog?…

Yup, that’s right!…in a public repo! 🙃 And so I didn’t write any blogs until I was super sure that I could write here.

But anyway - how y’all doing?! I’m good and I’m happy too because I recently made another account on Instagram where I only write and only speak Chamorro - you can see (and follow too if ya want) at i.dihan.dabit. There ain’t many posts yet, but I’ll definitely be posting more! 🫡

Also another thing: I’ll be starting my Masters in Computer Science program in the beginning of March 🤓. I’m really excited but a little worried too because I know I’m gon be busy!

Ahh man…guess it’s better to try and fail than to not try at all 😅.

That’s all for now. Much love! — DJT.

  1. This is how I’m translating “public repository”, e.g., my GitHub ↩︎
