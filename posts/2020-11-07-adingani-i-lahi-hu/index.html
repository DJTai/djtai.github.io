Talking To My Son

A note: this is a modified post from when I was blogging on write.as.

Most of the Chamoru that I’ve been using lately is towards i lahi-hu. Håcha na såkkan idåt-ña, pues inadingan-måmi ni ti mapput.

I wanted to share some of those phrases with you and perhaps get some feedback. If you’re here then I’ll assume you came from Twitter, IG, or some other social media site. Feel free to hit me up on any of those.

Nihi ta tutuhun.

Sinångan Siha ni’ Hu Sangåni i Lahi-hu

Phrases I Tell My Son

Chamoru English
Nihi ta tulaika i magågu-mu Let’s change your clothes
Nihi ta hånao Let’s go
Hånao! Huyung neni Go! Out neni
Hånao yan sodda’ si nanå-mu Go and find your mom
Håfa bidadå-mu? What are you doing?
Nå’i yu’ nu gi kannai-mu Give me what’s in your hand
Kao ñålang hao? Are you hungry?
Kao malagu’ hao chumochu? Do you want to eat?
Kao håspuk hao? Are you full?
Kao ma’o hao? Are you thirsty?
Håyi ennao/eyu? Who is that?
Maila’ mågi Come here
Nihi ta baila! Let’s dance!
Baila’ yan guahu Dance with me
Mungnga mangassas hao fan Don’t scratch yourself please
Mungnga ennao Don’t do that
Mungnga kumåti fan Please don’t cry
Sa’ håfa kumåkati hao neni? Why are you crying neni?
Atan yu’ Look at me
Kao matuhuk hao? Are you sleepy?
Kao esta yayas hao? Are you already tired?

Definitely simple phrases that I hope my son will catch on to. Gi minagåhet, hu tungu’ na ha kumprendi na ilek-hu ya ennao ha gof na’magof yu’.

Todu ha’ para på’gu.

Bula guinaiya — DJT.
