This Is The Way/To Be Like This

By David Taitingfong | February 03, 2022 | 2 minutes

Did you know that Chamorus used to follow a lunar calendar? I guess that shouldn’t come as a surprise when you realize just how connected seafaring peoples and cultures are to the stars and to the skies. Yet this was something that I had not learned until about 2 years ago.

For Chamorus the first month of the lunar calendar is Tumaiguini1 which translates to “this is the way” or “to be like this”. Traditionally this is a time of reflection; a time to look inward and to look ahead; to set your intentions for the coming year. Gi minag친het, si asagu친-hu yan gu친hu in keke’cho’gui esti. These past 2 weeks have been very promising and enlightening for us. We’ve both found ourselves reflecting on who we are, what we’ve accomplished, and we’ve been telling eachother what we hope to put into motion for this coming year. Personally, I feel really good; I feel relaxed and relieved and a bit energetic. I’m entering Tumaiguini with strong intentions. I’m telling myself to be better about my time; to be better at giving my partner more time (and reminding her to take more time) for herself; and to be better at setting boundaries in all areas of my life, particularly at work!

We’re both excited for this coming year. Setting plans isn’t something we always do. For me whenever I get the chance to, it usually doesn’t feel this good. I’m hopeful that by the summertime I’ll have made tremendous progress towards my goals. I hope you all can find the time to do the same.
